What Is Neuroplasticity?

The term was first used by Polish neuroscientist Jerzy Konorski in 1948 to describe observed changes in neuronal structure.
Neuroplasticity is the brains ability to change and adapt in response to experiences. It allows out brain to reorganise itself and create new neural connections. Changes in neuroplasticity can be observed through learning, but it can also be observed when the brain is damaged.

Neuroplasticity & Me

I am a life long learner. Learning new things is what fuels my mind and body. Neuroplasticity is important to me as I am constantly learning a vast array of topics, ranging from engineering to practical life skills. Neuroplasticity allows my brain to adapt and create new neural pathways when learning.

Increasing Neuroplasticity

There are a number of ways to improve your brains neuroplasticity. Some ways that work for one person, may not work for another but the core concepts tend to stay the same between everyone.

  • Use it or lose it: Always be training and regularly challenge yourself with new tasks to keep your brain active.

  • Adequate sleep: sleeping is your brains time to rest and recover. It is essential for brain health.

  • Reduce stress: being under chronic stress has been proven to damage the brain.

  • Physical health: Exercising and keeping active is not only beneficial to the body, but also for the mind.

What Is A Growth Mindset?

Growth mindset is the belief that intelligence is not determined by birth, but something that can be developed.
This is the opposite of a fixed mindset, which is the belief that your intelligence and abilities are fixed traits which you are born with.

Having a growth mindset is important as it can lead to increased motivation. If you believe that your abilities are not limited and you can improve with effort and practice, you are likely to see a large improvement.
fixed mindset, on the other hand, can discourage you, leading to giving up easily when facing a problem.

For me personally, I have always understood that level of success is down to the amount of effort you put in. Sure, there are limiting factors such as money, but your success in life will always reflected by the effort you put into it.
