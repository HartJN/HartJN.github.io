Identity & Values

By James Hart

Situation where you have made an ethical decision

I was out for a walk when I found a wallet on the ground. It was full of money, roughly $400 in notes, and no ID. While the extra money would have been nice, I knew ethically what I had to do. I went to the police station to hand it in, only to find the person who lost it, was there reporting it. They were extremely grateful, as it was their weeks whole paycheck and they would not of had enough money to pay for rent and food. Looking back at it, I would not hesitate to do the same thing as you never know what the other persons situation is.

How my culture and people around me have influenced my values and identity

My family have always been kind and respectful to others. This has directly influenced my values and how I identify, as I've always tried to treat others the same way. I have been taught to be considerate and thoughtful towards others.

My Strengths

Being a proactive problem solver, fast learner and team player will help my learning journey as a developer because it will allow me to identify and solve problems quickly, learn new concepts and technologies quickly and work well with others to achieve common goals.

Limitations of my learning

My writing skills are undoubtedly one of my weaknesses. I haven't written much recently, so my skills regressed.
I can see this affecting my learning journey in several ways. For one, it will mean that I have to work harder than others to express myself clearly in writing. This can be frustrating for me as I can have the knowledge but struggle to express what I mean.
Despite these challenges, I am confident that I can overcome them and still have a successful learning journey. This just means that I will need to put in the extra work to improve my writing skills.
