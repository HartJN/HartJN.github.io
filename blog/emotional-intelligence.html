Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence(EQ)?

Emotional intelligence is the ability to perceive, understand, and manage your emotions, and the emotions of others. The term was coined in 1990 in a research paper by two psychology professors, John D. Mayer of University of New Hampshire and Peter Saleovey of Yale.
The importance of the term was not fully recognised until 1998, where Rutgers psychologist Daniel Goleman wrote about it in an article for Harvard business review.

What is Intelligence quotient(IQ)?

IQ is a measure of a person's cognitive abilities and potential in comparison to others.
The measure if derived from one of several standardized tests designed to assess human intelligence. IQ is used as a predictor for academic and job performance.

How is it different to IQ?

When comparing people who have a high IQ but an average EQ and people who have a high EQ but an average intelligence, the people with higher EQs tend to be more successful in life. This is due to IQ only being a measure of ones intelligence, where EQ measures both cognitive and emotional ability.

The people who have high EQs have a better understanding of themselves and others, which allows them to be able to relate to people at a deeper level. Most of the Successful leaders tend to have high EQ, which allows them to motivate and inspire others.
