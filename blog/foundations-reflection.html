Foundations Reflections

What I Have Learnt About Myself

Throughout completing the foundation curriculum, I discovered new things about myself.

First I found that I am my own biggest critic. I would put off posting my work for others to see, as I could see all the flaws in my eyes, and it was not up to the standard that I set for myself. After posting my work, I found that I did not receive the negative criticism that my brain would tell me would happen and that it was all in my head.

I found that reflecting on what I have done, is a great way to retain what has been taught. Throughout the day, I write down notes of new things that I have learnt, or found interesting, and later in the day, I reflect on what I have written.
I have found that I have retained a lot more than in my previous study due to this.

Finally, I learnt what some of my weaknesses are, and what I need to work on to improve. I have made it a priority to focus on improving my human skills and to work on my weaknesses.

Values, Empathy, Self-Awareness & Programming

Collaboration is a big part of programming. In order for your team to succeed, you need to be able to communicate well with each other. Having good values, high empathy and self-awareness is what makes you succeed as a team player, as this gives you a deeper understanding of ourselves and others, allowing us to manage our emotions, stress and make more informed decisions.

Surprises & Challenges

During foundations, It was a bit of a shock to realise how much I'd been neglecting my own identity and values. For a long time, I had been focused on other things and I'd let my own needs and wants fall by the wayside.

Throughout this course, the core topics made me think deeply about who I am and what I stand for. It was hard to do at first but it made me realize that I need to make time for myself too. I need to take control of my own identity and make sure that my values are reflected in my everyday life.

Why Is Core Learning Important?

I believe that learning the core skills that Dev Academy covers, is very important and not a waste of time. From an industry point of view, web development is a rapidly changing field; the tools you use every day, may not be the same you use in a year.
However core skills are something that stays linear throughout your career.

Core topics such as Mindset, identity, and values are important for everyone because they help us understand our strengths and weaknesses, set personal and professional goals, and stay motivated throughout our careers.
Knowing these core skills is what separates good programmers from the best.
