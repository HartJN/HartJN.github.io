My Learning Plan

Updated Goals

During my time at Dev Academy, I've realised that my goals have changed. I used to want to be a specific title, like "software engineer" or "web developer". But now, I realise that my goal is to be able to present myself as a problem solver rather than a specific title.

I want to be able to use any programming technology to solve a problem I am given. I don't want to be limited to a specific language or framework. I want to be able to learn new technologies as they come out and quickly adapt to them.

My biggest strengths and limitations for Bootcamp

Bootcamp will be a great experience for me. I have a lot of strengths that I can bring to the table. However, I do have some limitations that I need to be aware of.
One of my biggest strengths is my past technical experience. This will give me a great foundation to build upon but I also understand that I do not know everything and will always accept teaching and feedback from others.

My main limitation would be that my self-criticism can sometimes hold me back and cause doubt. I need to be able to push through this and trust in my abilities.

My biggest non-technical challenge for Bootcamp

I've never been the most confident person when it comes to presenting my work to others, but that's something I've put on my priorities to learn.

I am often my own worst critic and I can be very hard on myself when it comes to my work. This can make it difficult to present my work to others, especially when I am not confident in what I have created.

Human skills I Would Like To Develop

One of the things I want to focus on improving is my ability to articulate my thoughts. I want to be able to explain my ideas clearly and concisely. I also want to be able to have discussions with other developers and be able to bounce ideas off of them.

I think that by improving my ability to communicate, I'll be able to improve my overall development skills. I'll be able to collaborate more effectively, and I'll be able to better understand the ideas of others. This will ultimately make me a better developer.

My Bootcamp Expectations

I am expecting an inclusive environment where everyone is working together to learn and grow. I know that I will receive feedback from my peers and the facilitators, and I am looking forward to using that feedback to improve my skills. I am also expecting to have good communication with the facilitators so that I can get the most out of the program.

Expectations Of Myself At Bootcamp

As I embark on my journey at Dev Academy Bootcamp, I have a few expectations of myself. Firstly, I expect to put 100% effort into each task. This means that I will give my all to every project and task, and not half-heartedly complete them.

Secondly, I will ask for help when needed. I know that there will be times when I am struggling and need assistance, so I will not hesitate to ask for help from my peers or facilitators.

Finally, I will be punctual. This means that I will attend each class on time and be prepared with any materials needed.

I expect these things of myself so that I can make the most of my time at Dev Academy and come out as a skilled and knowledgeable developer.

My long term goal

My ultimate career goal is becoming a full stack software developer.
I aim to be able to develop applications from scratch, from how the site looks, to how the site functions, and everything in-between. I want to be able to join a team of developers and be comfortable with the tasks that I am given.
I believe this is a realistic goal for me, as I understand that all it takes to achieve this is hard-work and consistency. Enspiral Dev Academy will help me achieve this.

My strengths and limitations of learning

My main strength when it comes to learning, is that I love a challenge. From a young age, I have always enjoyed taking things apart and putting them back together to see how they work. When it comes to learning, I find enjoy difficult situations where I have to really think about what I am doing, in order to achieve what I want.

One of my weaknesses is that I can be a bit of a workaholic. It can be an issue, as I can get so involved in my work, that I forgot about other commitments. This is something that I am working on, and will soon have a good understanding about how to balance work and life.

What skills I want to develop

One of the main reasons for me joining Dev Academy, was the range of core skills that they teach.
As my prior programming experience has been self taught, I have not had the experience of learning the human skills needed to become a great developer.
This is my main priority and I am really excited to start this journey and look back at how far I have come, once it is over.

How I will manage my workload

During my time at Dev Academy, I am making it a goal to manage my time well.
I will be using Notion to organise my time and to make sure that I am staying on track.
I also have a whiteboard that I write out my goals for the day and the challenges that I might come across.

Seeking Help

I am well aware of what burnout is, and I will not be putting myself in a position where that could occur. If I am struggling, I will be reaching out to my facilitators and peers for help and guidance.

What I expect from the Facilitation team

I expect a fun and safe learning environment, where everyone is included and feels comfortable seeking help.

Scheduling Information

I am in a position currently, where I can dedicate my time fully to studying at Dev Academy. Things may come up once in awhile, but I will always make sure that it is communicated to my facilitator.
